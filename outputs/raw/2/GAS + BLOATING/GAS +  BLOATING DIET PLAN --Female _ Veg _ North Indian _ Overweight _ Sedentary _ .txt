Early Morning (6:30–7:00 AM)
Dish: Warm Water + Lemon + Fennel Seeds + Ginger
● Ingredients: Warm water 200 ml, lemon juice 1 tsp, fennel seeds ½ tsp, grated ginger ¼
tsp
● Method: Boil fennel seeds and ginger in water for 3–5 min, strain, add lemon juice, drink
warm
● Time: 5 min
● Serving Size: 1 glass
● Nutritive Values: 20 kcal | 0.5 g protein | 3 g carbs | 0 g fat | 1 g fiber
Tip: Promotes digestion and relieves bloating. Avoid cold drinks in the morning.
Mid-Morning Snack (9:00–9:30 AM)
Option 1 – Papaya + Flax Seeds
● Ingredients: Papaya ½ cup (cubed), flax seeds 1 tsp
● Method: Mix papaya with flax seeds, eat fresh
● Time: 2 min
● Serving Size: ½ cup papaya + 1 tsp flax seeds
● Nutritive Values: 100 kcal | 1 g protein | 22 g carbs | 2 g fat | 4 g fiber
Option 2 – Cucumber + Carrot Sticks + Mint Yogurt Dip
● Ingredients: Cucumber ½ medium, carrot ½ medium, low-fat yogurt 2 tbsp, mint leaves
½ tsp
● Method: Cut cucumber and carrot into sticks, mix yogurt with mint, dip and eat
● Time: 5 min
● Nutritive Values: 120 kcal | 5 g protein | 12 g carbs | 3 g fat | 4 g fiber

Tip: Hydrating, low-FODMAP snacks reduce bloating.
Breakfast (11:00 AM)
Option 1 – Moong Dal Chilla + Mint Chutney + Sprouts Salad
● Ingredients: Moong dal ½ cup (soaked 2–3 hr), mint 1 tsp, water as needed, oil ½ tsp,
sprouts ¼ cup
● Method: Grind moong dal with water into a smooth batter. Heat a non-stick pan, lightly
grease with oil, make 2 chillas. Serve with mint chutney and sprouts salad.
● Time: 20 min
● Serving Size: 2 chillas + 2 tbsp chutney + ¼ cup sprouts
● Nutritive Values: 350 kcal | 18 g protein | 40 g carbs | 7 g fat | 6 g fiber
Option 2 – Ragi Porridge with Steamed Apple + Chia Seeds
● Ingredients: Ragi flour ¼ cup, water ½ cup, apple grated/steamed ¼ cup, chia seeds 1
tsp
● Method: Boil water, add ragi flour slowly, cook 5–7 min, top with apple and chia seeds
● Time: 10 min
● Nutritive Values: 300 kcal | 7 g protein | 50 g carbs | 5 g fat | 6 g fiber
Tip: Cooked grains are gentle on the digestive system; avoid raw high-FODMAP foods.
Lunch (1:30–2:00 PM)
Option 1 – Steamed Rice + Moong Dal + Lauki Curry + Curd
● Ingredients: Rice ½ cup, moong dal 2 tbsp, bottle gourd ¼ cup, oil ½ tsp, curd 3 tbsp
● Method: Cook rice and dal separately. Sauté bottle gourd lightly, add dal, cook 5–7 min.
Serve with curd.
● Time: 25 min

● Serving Size: 1 bowl rice + ½ katori dal + ¼ katori curry + 3 tbsp curd
● Nutritive Values: 450 kcal | 20 g protein | 55 g carbs | 8 g fat | 6 g fiber
Option 2 – Quinoa + Paneer + Steamed Vegetables
● Ingredients: Quinoa ½ cup, paneer 50 g, carrots + beans ¼ cup, oil ½ tsp
● Method: Cook quinoa, lightly sauté vegetables with oil, add paneer cubes
● Time: 25 min
● Nutritive Values: 470 kcal | 22 g protein | 50 g carbs | 9 g fat | 6 g fiber
Tip: Use cooked, soft vegetables and mild spices to reduce bloating.

Dietary & Cultural Context
● Vegetarian, North Indian staples, mild spices, low-FODMAP
● Focus on warm, cooked foods to relieve bloating
● Avoid: raw cruciferous vegetables, beans, heavy fried foods
● Emphasis on small meals, hydration, and probiotics
Rationale & Alternatives
● Protein: Moong dal, paneer, soy milk → satiety, muscle maintenance
● Carbs: Rice, ragi, quinoa → low glycemic, reduce bloating
● Digestive spices: Fennel, ginger, mint → improve digestion, reduce gas
● Alternatives: Paneer ↔ Tofu, Ragi ↔ Oats/Bajra, Mint ↔ Coriander, Curd ↔ Soy
Yogurt
Tips for Gas & Bloating (Sedentary)
1. Eat small, frequent meals
2. Chew slowly; avoid gulping air
3. Drink warm water or herbal teas
4. Include probiotic/fermented foods

5. Avoid raw beans, cabbage, cauliflower, carbonated drinks
6. Gentle post-meal walk (5–10 min) improves digestion
