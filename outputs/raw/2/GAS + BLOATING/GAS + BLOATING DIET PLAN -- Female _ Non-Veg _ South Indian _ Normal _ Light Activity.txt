GAS + BLOATING DIET PLAN
Profile: Female | Non-Veg | South Indian | Normal | Light Activity
Client / User Profile:
● Age: 27 years
● Sex: Female
● Height: 160 cm
● Weight: 55–58 kg
● BMI: Normal
● Activity Level: Light (walking, light yoga, 2–3 workouts/week)
● Diet Type: Non-Vegetarian
● Cultural Preference: South Indian
● Health Focus: Reduce bloating and gas, support digestion, maintain healthy weight
Overall Daily Nutritional Range:
● Calories: 1800–1900 kcal
● Protein: 65–70 g
● Carbohydrates: 200–210 g
● Fat: 55–60 g
● Fiber: 28–32 g
● Sodium: < 2000 mg/day

Early Morning (6:30–7:00 AM)
Dish: Warm Water + Lemon + Fennel Seeds + Ginger
● Ingredients: Warm water 200 ml, lemon juice 1 tsp, fennel seeds ½ tsp, grated ginger ¼
tsp
● Method: Boil fennel seeds and ginger in water for 3–5 min, strain, add lemon juice, drink
warm
● Time: 5 min
● Serving Size: 1 glass
● Nutritive Values: 20 kcal | 0.5 g protein | 3 g carbs | 0 g fat | 1 g fiber
● Tip: Promotes digestion and reduces bloating. Avoid cold drinks in the morning.
Breakfast (8:00–8:30 AM)
Option 1 – Ragi Dosa + Coconut Chutney + Herbal Tea
● Ingredients: Ragi flour ½ cup, urad dal 2 tbsp, grated coconut 1 tbsp, oil ½ tsp
● Method: Ferment batter, pan-fry dosas, serve with chutney and tea
● Time: 20 min
● Serving Size: 2 dosas + 2 tbsp chutney + 1 cup tea
● Nutritive Values: 420 kcal | 18 g protein | 45 g carbs | 10 g fat | 5 g fiber
● Tip: Use minimal oil, chew slowly to reduce bloating
Option 2 – Vegetable Upma + Boiled Egg (1)
● Ingredients: Semolina ½ cup, carrot 2 tbsp, beans 2 tbsp, oil ½ tsp, boiled egg 1
● Method: Roast semolina, cook with vegetables and water, serve with boiled egg
● Time: 15 min

● Serving Size: 1 bowl upma + 1 egg
● Nutritive Values: 430 kcal | 20 g protein | 48 g carbs | 9 g fat | 5 g fiber
● Tip: Avoid excess salt, add a pinch of asafoetida for digestion
Option 3 – Moong Dal Chilla + Mint Chutney + Green Tea
● Ingredients: Moong dal ½ cup, mint 1 tbsp, ginger ½ tsp, oil ½ tsp
● Method: Grind dal, make pancakes, serve with chutney and tea
● Time: 20 min
● Serving Size: 2 chillas + 2 tbsp chutney + 1 cup tea
● Nutritive Values: 440 kcal | 21 g protein | 42 g carbs | 8 g fat | 6 g fiber
● Tip: Moong dal is easy to digest, helps reduce gas

● Method: Serve raw
● Time: 2 min

● Time: 5 min
● Serving Size: 1 small plate

● Method: Boil rice, cook fish with minimal oil and spices, steam vegetables
● Time: 25 min
● Serving Size: 1 bowl rice + ½ katori curry + ¼ katori vegetables
● Nutritive Values: 560 kcal | 30 g protein | 50 g carbs | 12 g fat | 6 g fiber
Option 2 – Millet Pongal + Chicken Saute + Buttermilk

glass
● Method: Cook millet and dal, sauté chicken lightly, serve with buttermilk
● Time: 25 min

Dietary & Cultural Context
● Allergens: Dairy, Nuts, Gluten, Egg
● Diet Type: Non-Vegetarian
● Cultural Preference: South Indian
● Sodium Restriction: < 2000 mg/day
● Focus on easily digestible meals to reduce bloating and gas
Rationale & Alternatives
● Designed for normal-weight females with light activity experiencing gas and bloating
● Promotes anti-inflammatory and digestion-friendly foods (ginger, fennel, mint, tulsi)
● Includes protein from dal, paneer, egg, fish to maintain muscle and satiety
● Emphasizes hydration, fiber, and low-fat cooking to reduce bloating
● Substitutions:
○ Fish ↔ Chicken
○ Paneer ↔ Tofu
○ Milk ↔ Soy Milk

○ Ragi ↔ Foxtail Millet or Oats
