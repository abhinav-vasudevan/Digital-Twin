DIETARY & CULTURAL CONTEXT
Diet Type: Non-Vegetarian
Cuisine Style: South Indian
Cooking Oil: Cold-pressed coconut oil, groundnut oil, sesame oil
Preferred Flavors: Mild–medium spice, moderate salt
Allergens: Eggs, dairy (optional), nuts (adjust as required)
Cultural Fit: Includes South-Indian staples such as dosa, idli, poha, ragi, millet, chicken, fish,
and coconut-based preparations
Focus: Muscle gain + healthy weight gain + balanced high-protein high-fiber meals
Special Considerations:
• Heavy activity → higher calorie & protein meals
• Underweight → energy-dense good fats (nuts, seeds, coconut, ghee)
• Easy-to-digest foods to prevent bloating
RATIONALE & ALTERNATIVES
Rationale
This diet plan is designed to:
• Increase calorie intake safely to support weight gain
• Provide high protein (110–125 g/day) to build lean muscle

• Provide high fiber to support gut health and slow digestion for steady energy
• Support heavy activity levels with balanced carbs and fats
• Include easy South Indian foods for long-term adherence
• Prevent nutrient deficiencies (iron, B12, protein, calcium)
• Improve metabolism and maintain stable energy throughout the day
Alternatives
Protein swaps:
• Chicken ↔ Fish ↔ Eggs ↔ Soya chunks ↔ Paneer (if not strict vegan)
Carb swaps:
• Brown rice ↔ Red rice ↔ Millets ↔ Quinoa ↔ Wheat chapati
Fiber swaps:
• Oats ↔ Ragi ↔ Millets ↔ Vegetable stir-fries ↔ Sprouts
Healthy fat swaps:
• Almonds ↔ Walnuts ↔ Peanuts ↔ Pumpkin seeds ↔ Coconut
Drink swaps:
• Coconut water ↔ Buttermilk ↔ Lemon ginger water
Snack swaps:
• Dates ↔ Raisins ↔ Banana ↔ Sweet potato
