Dietary & Cultural Context

Cuisine Style: South Indian flavours (dosa, idli, sambar, rasam adapted with millets/quinoa)
Cooking Oil: Cold-pressed mustard / groundnut / minimal olive oil (max 1–2 tsp/day)
Preferred Flavours: Mild spice, turmeric, cumin, curry leaves, fresh herbs
Allergens: Dairy (paneer, milk, curd), soy (tofu, soy milk), nuts (almonds), gluten (if wheat used
in rotis) — adjust per tolerance
Tips for Weight Loss + PCOS
1. Prioritize protein at every meal (lentils, paneer/tofu, soy, millets).
2. Choose low-GI grains (millets, quinoa, steel-cut oats, brown rice).
3. Space meals every 3–4 hours; avoid long fasting that triggers cravings.
4. Post-meal walk (15–20 min) to improve insulin sensitivity.
5. Avoid refined sugar, packaged snacks, fruit juices.
6. Include anti-inflammatory spices daily (turmeric, cinnamon, cumin).
7. Sleep 7–8 hours and manage stress (yoga, breathing).
8. Monitor portion sizes—use measured bowls/glasses (below).
9. Strength training 2–3x/week to preserve lean mass.
10. Track weight and symptoms (acne, cycles) every 2 weeks.
Rationale & Alternatives
• Heavy activity increases energy needs — plan provides a modest calorie deficit while
supporting workout recovery.
• High protein + fiber + low-GI carbs improve satiety and insulin control in PCOS.
• Anti-inflammatory ingredients (turmeric, cinnamon, nuts, omega-rich seeds) support hormonal
balance.
Alternatives:

● Paneer ↔ Tofu
● Brown rice ↔ Quinoa ↔ Foxtail millet
● Moong dal ↔ Masoor dal ↔ Horse gram (kulthi)
● Skim milk ↔ Soy milk ↔ Almond milk (unsweetened)
● Makhana ↔ Roasted chana ↔ Roasted peanuts (adjust calories)
